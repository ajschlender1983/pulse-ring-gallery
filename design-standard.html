The Pulse ring & the Pulse ripple.

One character, rendered identically everywhere. This is the standard every still, film, and render must follow — the object's exact anatomy (now including the motor flat-spot), and the rules for the one branded ripple.

1 · The ring

exact anatomy

A wide, low-profile mirror-polished band with a matte ivory-white inner sleeve. The signature the samples were missing: the inner sleeve is not a perfect circle — it has one small flat straight segment where the vibration motor sits. Visible any time you can see into the opening.

Band
Wide, low-profile, wider than it is thick. Softly rounded edges, gently curved outer wall — never a bulbous dome.
Outer surface
One continuous flawless mirror-polished metal. No window, screen, seam, logo, or button.
Inner sleeve
Matte ivory-white, at the inner edge only. One flat segment (motor). Never a perfect circle.
Colorways
Champagne gold   Silver / platinum
Lighting
The ring is a solid object. It never glows or lights from the inside.
Never
Thin band · fat dome · dark inset/screen · sensor nub · perfectly round inner sleeve · interior glow.

2 · The ripple — exploration

6 implementations

The felt vibration, made visible. Six treatments on the same ring, so the only variable is the ripple. Reference point: the original site's hero, where the whole scene gently ripples like water disturbed by a drop.

3 · The Pulse ripple — the standard

the rule
The Pulse ripple is warm-gold, translucent, concentric waves that emanate outward from the ring's outer edge and gently ripple the surrounding scene — like the surface of still water disturbed by a single drop. Soft, slow, breathing. It lives around the ring, never inside it.

Always

  • Emanate from the ring's outer edge, expanding outward and fading
  • Warm gold → soft cream (#D9B45E core, fading to warm light)
  • Concentric and even, like rings on a pond — one clear wave, or a slow set of them
  • Translucent and soft; the scene stays visible through the ripple
  • Let it gently distort/ripple the scene (lifestyle) or the surface (product)
  • In motion: slow, one calm pulse every few seconds — a breath, not a strobe

Never

  • Glow or emit from inside the ring — the band is never a light source
  • Look like literal water/liquid the ring is sitting in (treatment B)
  • Cool, blue, neon, or electric — it is always warm and organic
  • Harsh, sharp, or high-contrast; no lens-flare fireworks
  • Flood the whole frame with a formless glow (over-bloom, treatment F)
  • Originate from a point or a screen — it is a wave, not a beam

One ripple, everywhere

Treatment A — concentric warm light-rings — is the single Pulse ripple, used in every context, product and lifestyle alike, for maximum brand consistency. The scene-ripple (E) and single-ring (D) are occasional variants of the exact same wave — reach for them only when a specific lifestyle scene or a minimal UI moment calls for it. The idea never changes: warm concentric waves from the ring's outer edge.

Standard locked

Ripple = treatment A, everywhere. Ring = wide low-profile band, mirror polish, ivory sleeve with the flat motor-spot, never lit from inside. Every ring asset is being re-rendered to this full standard, and the shareable gallery updated.
